3.     Standard Deviation (S.D)

It is the square root of the arithmetic mean of the square of the deviation from the mean.

Steps involved:

1.     Step I: Find the arithmetic mean

2.     Step II: Subtract the arithmetic mean from each of the variable.

3.     Step III: Square each of the deviation from the mean.

4.     Step IV: Find the sum of the squares of the deviation and divide the sum by the number of variables.

5.     Step V: Take the square root of the result from step IV and you have the standard deviation.
