SOUND WAVES

Sound wave is a wave produced wherever an elastic medium is disturbed. It does not travel through a vacuum bit it travels through a solid, liquid and gorgeous media. Hence it is a mechanical wave due to the fact that its wave motion is parallel to its particle motion. Then, it is longitudinal in nature.

A unit propagated Sound Wave is called a Note, while combination of notes of regular frequency results to tone while notes of irregular frequency result to noise. The normal frequency of a sound wave falls within 20Hz to 20 KHz, which is referred to as Normal level of Audibility. But, sound wave whose frequency is below 20Hz is the infrasonic sound with Low level of Audibility. And also, sound wave whose frequency is above 20KHz is termed ultrasonic sound with High level of Audibility.

A note is said to be the octave of another note if it has twice.

Wave Terminology

Under the heading, those terms associated with wave will be discussed:

a.     Amplitude

b.     Period

c.     Frequency

d.     Wavelength

e.     Angular velocity

f.     Propagation constant

g.     Phase Angle

h.     Wave speed

Amplitude: This is the maximum displacement through which a propagating wave can be displaced. It is denoted with symbol 'A' and measured in metre.

Period: This is the time taken to complete an oscillation. It is denoted with symbol 'T' and measured in 'second'.

Frequency: This is the number of oscillations covered by a propagating wave within a second. It is denotes with symbol 'f' and measured in 'Hertz' i.e. Hz in honour of a physicist called Heinrich Hertz. It is mathematically the reciprocal of period.


Wavelength: This is the distance moved by a propagating wave to complete an oscillation or the distance between two successive crests or two successive troughs. It is denoted with symbol 'Lambda (λ)' and is measured in metre.

Angular Velocity: This is the rate of change of angular displacement of a propagating wave with respect to the time taken. It is denoted with symbol 'w' and measured in 'rads-1'.

Propagation Constant: This is the ratio of angular displacement to the linear displacement of a propagating wave. It can be otherwise called WAVE CONSTANT and it is denoted with symbol 'K' and measured in 'rads-1'.

Phase Angle: This is the angular displacement that account for how much the wave has been shifted from the equilibrium position. A wave whose starting point is at the origin has its phase angle to be ZERO. It is denoted with symbol 'ϕ' in 'rad'.

                                             

Wave Speed: This is the distance travelled by a propagating wave per unit time along the direction of flow of energy.It is denoted with symbol with symbol 'v' and measured in 'ms-1'
