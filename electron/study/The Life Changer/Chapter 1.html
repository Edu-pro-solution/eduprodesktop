Chapter One

Ummi watches her daughters as they await their father's return from the only bank in the village where he works as an accountant. She watches on in admiration as her youngest child, Bint, narrates to her siblings the uproar she causes when she asks her Social Studies teacher about a question in French which he didn't have an answer to. Ummi, being a teacher, joins in the conversation, urging jokingly that the man be given a break. They all share a close family bond filled with morals, respect and fun.


Omar, the first child and only son announces to the family that he has been offered an admission to the Ahmadu Bello University, Zaria, to study Law. Ummi believes her husband will be thrilled and there will be a grand celebration, as Law is his passion before circumstances made him study Accountancy instead. Omar urges his siblings to allow him the opportunity to break the news to his father himself, as he has been promised a new android phone if he secures the admission. The news of Omar's success is received with cheers and teasing while they all move to the front of the house, waiting for Omar's father.


They start discussing amidst rounds of Zobo drinks when Ummi is asked to tell a story. She thinks about her son's latest achievement and the changes this will bring to his life. He will have to move to a new environment where there is enough freedom, people from all walks of life, and a lot of other experiences. She decides to tell Omar and his siblings the story of her university days and how it changed her, hoping it will serve as a didactic piece to the 18 year old boy who is about to start another phase of his life.
