His landlord, Mr Ogunwale, wife and grand children Jide and Kemi had a cordial relationship with him, so much so the landlord volunteered to drive him to the airport in his Honda Pilot. In his free time he has taught Jide, the other of the two grandchildren Elocution and African History. While preparing to travel, he had made sure to pack some Nigerian groceries, sold his Pathfinder SUV for 1.5 million to Jeremi Amos, the accountant and gifted his freezer and other electronics to his landlady. At least four of Stardom's teachers offered to see him off to Murtala Mohammed airport. Donning a white T-shirt and black jeans, he prepared to travel after his company had left. He soon dozed off and dreamt of slavery.


Back at Stardom, he is sorely missed and while the whole school was trying to adjust to a new life without him, he emerges again, saying he won't be leaving and loved it there. This prompts a rousing victory song of the school which concludes the story.
