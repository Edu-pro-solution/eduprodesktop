Chapter Ten : Passport Pains




Bepo’s documents had expired but he was not keen on renewal due to issues associated with passport acquisition in the country. However his family mounted pressure on him by the first quarter of 2022. Nevertheless, he still was scared of the hassles and cutting corners to get the job done especially in Lagos. So he went to Ibadan because he surmised that the stress would be less compared to that of Lagos. Even when the government gave assurance of how one could stresslessly get their passports, speculations remained, especially because of the perceived insincerity of government and the activities of middlemen.


Bepo's agent was a man named Tai whom he met on Wednesday having travelled from the Ojota park in Lagos and travelling through the pundit-perceived ‘most important project in the country’, the Lagos Ibadan expressway.


He wondered about many government schemes and policies and the irony in the fact that the country seemed religious. He also mused about JP Clark's ‘Ibadan’ poem and the time the school came on excursion.
