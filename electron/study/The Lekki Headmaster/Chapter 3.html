Chapter Three : Migration Tales

Bepo considered UK, US and Canada for his relocation and surmised that the hourly wage paid abroad was better for both employers and employees compared to the monthly salaries in Nigeria. His findings revealed that skilled work paid off more and the focus of the migrating many was the conversion rate in naira when they earned in foreign currencies. The brain drain which was from all walks of life led to the desperation of many including Mr Nku fled who with 2 million from the school's cooperative.

Sola, the Home Economics teacher nevertheless affirmed that it was ‘safe’ for him to relocate due to the fact he already had a family over. Chief William’s death and how his children couldn't continue due the family being unable to continue the fees highlighted how expensive quality education was in Nigeria. Chapter 3 basically exposes us to the details and mixed fortunes that befall those caught up in the japa syndrome and their coping mechanisms. Stories of characters like Sola and Betty, her asthmatic child and Riike are highlighted to emphasize this. Better infrastructure and quality education are highlighted as main reasons for the mass emigration.
